# Positioning: why this exists, and why you're the one building it

**Date:** July 20 2026
**Problem this solves:** the site currently has no answer to "who made this and why should I trust them," which is the first question a skeptical senior professional asks. The About page is a bio. It should be the argument.

---

## 1. The actual differentiator

Most AI courses are taught by AI people. That is a weakness disguised as a credential, because an AI person cannot see what a non-AI person doesn't know.

You are not an AI person selling AI. **You are an operator who has watched four companies fail at the same thing, in four different industries, and recognised the pattern about to repeat.** Tesla, Expedia, Highmark, Barneys. Manufacturing, travel, healthcare, luxury retail. Nothing in common except the crack in the floor.

That is a far stronger position than expertise, because it is not a claim about knowing AI. It is a claim about knowing *organisations*, which your audience of senior professionals will recognise instantly and cannot dismiss.

The thesis in one line:

> **AI isn't going to fail people because it's too complicated. It's going to fail them the same way every system did: because nobody explained the foundation.**

## 2. The copy

>
> Tesla. Expedia. Highmark. Barneys.
>
> Manufacturing, travel, healthcare, luxury retail. Different decades, different problems, different rooms. And underneath all four, the same thing: teams moving fast on top of foundations nobody had been taught.

> Not because people weren't smart. The people were extraordinary. It was that no one had ever explained the fundamentals in language that made sense to the people actually doing the work. So the fundamentals got skipped, and everything built on top of them inherited the gap.
>

>

>

## 3. Where it goes

**Not a page nobody visits.** The About page is where credibility goes to die. This belongs in the path of the skeptic's first visit.

| Placement | Treatment | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Home, first visit, below the Level 0 card** | Short form, 3 sentences, links to long | The skeptic reads it before deciding whether to spend two minutes |
| **Level 0 opener, as a foreword** | Long form, set as a foreword rather than a bio | Level 0 is about lowering the guard. Your credentials are the reason to lower it |
| **Field-note units** | First person, already the case | You are in the content, not adjacent to it |
| **Footer, every page** | One line | Quiet, constant |
| **`/about/`** | Long form plus the four companies | For the person who goes looking |

**The foreword placement is the strongest.** Concept A treats it as a foreword to the manual, which is exactly the right register: not "here's my LinkedIn," but "here's why this document exists."

---

## 4. Writing notes

**Name the companies, don't lean on them.** The power is in the *pattern across four industries*, not in the logos. "Tesla, Expedia, Highmark, Barneys" as a flat list reads as a résumé. "Manufacturing, travel, healthcare, luxury retail, and the same crack in the floor" reads as an insight.

**Don't claim AI expertise.** The moment you position as an AI expert you are competing with people who have more of it, on a dimension your audience cares less about. Stay the translator. It is the rarer and more useful thing.

**Say the uncomfortable part.** "I keep meeting brilliant, senior people who have quietly decided AI isn't for them." That sentence is the whole product. It names the reader's private position without insulting them, which is the same move Level 0 makes.

**No em-dashes**, per house style. The copy above complies.

**Spanish:** translate the pattern, not the phrasing. "La misma grieta en el suelo" works in both markets. Company names stay as-is.

## 5. Open

1. **How much career detail?** Titles and dates make it a résumé. Zero detail makes it unverifiable. Suggest naming industries and the recurring role, without titles.
2. **A photo?** For a trust-first product aimed at people who are guarded, probably yes, once. Not on every page.
3. **First-gen and bilingual background.** It is real, it is relevant to who this course reaches, and it is your call whether it belongs in the foreword or stays in the fuller About.
