Five palette directions

Colour was doing the wrong job. It was making the product feel gentle, and you are not selling gentle.

The current palette reads soft and reassuring, which fights the thing you are actually offering: rigour, from someone who has watched four companies skip the fundamentals. Warm and reassuring is what a wellness app looks like. You want warm and exacting.

Each direction below comes from something real about you rather than from a trend board. All five clear WCAG AA on body text, muted text, accent, and button labels. Same layout throughout, so you are judging colour and nothing else.

A

Barro & Marigold, corrected

Your palette, fixed rather than replaced. The problem was never the hue, it was the haze. The old paper is a yellowed beige and the old ink is a soft brown, so everything sits in a narrow tonal band and nothing feels sharp. Cooler paper, near-black ink, deeper clay. Same identity, suddenly legible. Barro stays, and it should: clay is the oldest building material there is.

What I'd pick, and why

C, Talavera. It is the only one of the five that is simultaneously unmistakably yours, not a tech palette, and psychologically right for a guarded reader. Blue does something none of the warm options can: it reads institutional and verifiable, which is the register your fact-governance work is already reaching for.
