The redesign · Studio

Authority through showing your work, not through polish.

Every previous version tried to earn trust by looking clean. That is the same move every AI product makes, and it is the reason the reviewer's senior colleague would bounce off it. This earns trust the opposite way: by being visibly made by a person who thought hard about the drawing.

Fraunces and Inter stay, as you asked. But Fraunces is now running its SOFT and WONK axes, which is what that typeface was built for and what the old build never touched. Same fonts, completely different voice.

01

The diagram is the product

Your analogies are all drawable, and none of them have ever been drawn. A gas tank rendered as a hand-made technical plate does more for retention, and more for credibility, than any amount of typographic restraint.

Fig. 1 — The tank

Drawn once, reused everywhere: the plate, the unit screen, the glossary entry for token, and the share card. One asset, four jobs.

Why this is the whole redesign. A drawn diagram cannot be mistaken for generated output, cannot be produced by a template, and does the actual pedagogical work your text is currently doing alone. It is also the cheapest possible signal that a person made this on purpose.

Marks, rationed

A drawn underline for emphasis, a kraft highlight for a definition, and nothing else.

The annotation voice is Fraunces italic in red pencil. Margin notes only, never instruction.

Palette

Newsprint, graphite, editor's red, blueprint blue, kraft, checked-green. Every one is a real workshop material, and the red and blue both mean something: correction and plans.

Surfaces

An index card, taped down, one degree off square. Not a div with a border radius.

The one discipline that keeps this from becoming kitsch: marks are rationed. One drawn underline per screen, one plate per unit, one piece of tape per card. A workshop is orderly. A scrapbook is not, and the difference is the entire risk here.

If this is the direction, the next move is drawing the other 25 analogies. That's the real work, and it's the part nobody can copy.
