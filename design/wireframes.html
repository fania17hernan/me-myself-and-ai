Where “Worth another look” comes from

When Duolingo replaced its skill tree with the linear path, it gained completion and lost “cracked skills,” the cue showing which skills had gone rusty. It traded targeted review for linear clarity.

You don't have to make that trade. Phase 0's firstTry field already records which questions each learner missed on their first attempt, and it's immutable, so a retake can't paper over it. That data is sitting there unused.

Shown one unit at a time, never as a list of failures.

The cheat sheet

Built from takeaway lines you already wrote 26 times. They're currently decoration. Collected and exportable, they become the thing someone actually carries into work on Monday, which is also why it's a distribution channel.
06

Navigation · what the hamburger holds

It exists. It just stops hiding things people need to find.

Feature discovery drops 30 to 50% for items behind a hamburger, because out of sight reads as nonexistent. Apple's HIG and Material both recommend tabs for 3 to 5 primary destinations. So primary lives in the thumb zone, and the hamburger takes what's genuinely occasional.

Why four, and why these four

Home Resume, path, worth another look. The path gets no tab of its own, because Home is the path and duplicating it wastes one of four slots.
Use It Profession entry. Belongs in primary precisely because it's the answer to “I don't know where to start.”
Glossary The non-learning use case. Someone mid-workday who needs one definition and nothing else.
You Capabilities, cheat sheet, review. Where proof-over-points lives.
Spanish labels: Inicio · Aplícalo · Glosario · Tu avance. “Tu avance” is two words where the others are one, which is a live problem for a tab bar. Worth solving before build.
07

The content model

Three levels of structure where there are currently two.
Level  5, each a visual edition    L0 … L4
Topic  26, the modules you have
Unit   ~75, 2–3 min, ONE idea
The unit is the atom. It's what the path lists, what progress counts, what a URL points at, and what someone can finish standing in a queue. Topics stop being things you do and become things you have done.

08

Open before build

1. How do ~75 units get authored? One file per unit is 150 files across both languages. Hand-authoring that is exactly how the current 71-file problem started. Recommend generating units from markdown, which also turns the Spanish mirror into a translation task instead of a duplication task.
2. Do units need their own URLs? Yes, for sharing and for the glossary to link into. But that's the same decision as #1, not a separate one.
3. “Tu avance” is two words. Every other tab label is one. Needs solving before build.
4. Does /learn/ need a tab after all? Watch whether people go looking for a browse view that Home doesn't obviously provide.
5. Level 0 placement. The right first stop for a skeptic, but a returning learner must never be pushed back into it.
