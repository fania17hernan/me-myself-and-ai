Tokens & Rate Limits: The Gas Tank

Why does AI cut off mid-answer? Why did it just… stop? By the end of this module you'll know exactly why, and how to never get stranded again.

In this module

  • One analogy you'll never forget, the gas tank
  • What a "token" actually is, in plain English
  • Three habits that make AI cheaper, faster and less frustrating

The problem

It died mid-sentence.

True story: I was deep in a real project, on a roll, AI assistant doing exactly what I needed. I pasted in one more big chunk of work and hit enter.

Nothing. Then a timeout error. Then, when it finally answered, it stopped mid-sentence, like someone yanked the cord out of the wall.

You probably assumed you did something wrong.

Neither you nor the tool was broken. Something very predictable happened, and once you can see it, you can work around it every single time.

The core analogy

Tokens are gas in a tank.

Every word you type burns a little gas. Every word the AI writes back burns gas too. When the tank hits empty the car stops, even mid-sentence, even on the highway. And rate limits? That's the gas station that only lets you fill up so many times an hour. Come back too often and they make you wait.

That's the whole mental model. Everything else in this module is just detail about how big the tank is and how the gas gets burned.

How it works

What's actually in the tank

Four things worth knowing. None of them require you to do maths.

A token is a chunk of a word

A token is the unit AI actually reads and writes. Roughly three-quarters of a word, so 100 words is about 130 tokens. Close enough: words ≈ tokens.

Both directions burn gas

Here's what surprises people: it's not just what you type. Input, meaning your message plus everything earlier in the conversation, and output, the AI's reply, both count. Ask a short question that needs a long answer? That burns plenty of fuel too.

Long conversations get slow and pricey

Each time you send a message, the AI re-reads the whole conversation so far. Message 40 in a long chat burns far more than message 2. You're hauling the entire trip history with you.

Every car has a different tank

Claude, ChatGPT and Gemini each have different tank sizes and different gas-station rules, and they change them often. The sizes don't matter. The behaviour does: every tool has a limit, and every tool acts strange in the same way when you hit it.

What "empty" looks like

  • Truncation. The answer cuts off mid-sentence. Out of gas.
  • Timeout or error. It gives up before finishing. Engine flooded.
  • "Try again later." You hit the rate limit. The gas station is telling you to come back in an hour.

What this means for you

Drive like you know there's a tank

  1. Break big tasks into trips

    Don't paste a 40-page document and ask for everything at once. Ask for the summary first. Then the risks. Then the rewrite. Three short trips beat one stalled-out haul.

  2. Start fresh when you change topics

    A new chat is a full tank. If you're done with the budget analysis and moving to the team meeting agenda, open a new conversation instead of dragging the old one along.

  3. When it cuts off, just say "continue"

    Truncation isn't failure, the tank ran dry mid-answer. Reply "please continue" and it picks up where it stopped. Works almost every time.

Try it this week

Take one big task you'd normally dump in a single message and split it into three smaller asks. Notice the difference in speed and quality.

Say it to a colleague

AI has a gas tank. Long conversations burn more fuel. Break big tasks into trips.

That's the whole module in one sentence. If you can explain it to someone at work tomorrow, it stuck. Next, three quick questions. No grades, just proof it landed.
