AI 101: The Airport

You've heard of ChatGPT, Claude, maybe Gemini. But what actually are they, why are there so many, and what can they really do on an ordinary day? Start here.

In this module

  • What an “AI model” actually is, in plain words
  • Why there are so many AI products, and who the big ones are
  • What AI can do in a normal day, beyond search

The problem

“So… which one am I even supposed to use?”

You've typed a question into ChatGPT, maybe to reword a text or look something up. It felt like a smarter search box. Then you hear about Claude, Gemini, Copilot, and you wonder if you're missing out, or doing it wrong.

Before any of the details, let's zoom out and see the whole picture. No experience needed.

The core analogy

The Airport

Think of AI like air travel. An AI model is the aircraft, the powerful engine that carries you somewhere you couldn't easily reach on your own. The products you've heard of, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, are the airlines: different companies, each flying their own planes, competing for your ticket.

And the destinations? Everything AI can help you do. You don't need to understand jet engines to fly. You just need to know where you want to go.

That's the whole landscape: many airlines, many planes, countless destinations, and you, holding a ticket you didn't realize you had.

How it actually works

The landscape, in plain words

An AI model (in plain words: the “brain” that responds) is trained on enormous amounts of text and images to spot patterns and answer in everyday language. It's the aircraft, not the airline.

Why there are so many

It's a new, fast-moving industry. Big companies each built their own, so they compete on price, speed, and personality. More competition means better and cheaper for you.

The major airlines (today)

ChatGPT (OpenAI), Claude (Anthropic), Gemini (Google), Copilot (Microsoft). They change often, so the names matter less than knowing they're alternatives that do similar things.

They're more alike than different

For everyday tasks, any of them gets you there. Pick one, get comfortable, and you can always switch airlines later.

What this means for you

Where AI could take you

1. It's not just a search box

Ask it to reword an awkward email, summarize a long article, plan a weekend, explain something confusing in simple terms, or brainstorm ideas. That's the part most people never try.

2. Start with one real thing this week

Pick a small task you'd normally do by hand and hand it to AI instead. Notice how it feels.

3. Don't agonize over the “right” one

Any of the major ones is a fine first flight. You're choosing an airline, not getting married.

Where could it take you?

Jot down 3 things you do that involve writing, looking something up, planning, or explaining. Those are your first destinations.

One takeaway

Say it to a colleague

AI models are aircraft; the products (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot) are airlines. You don't need to know how the engine works, just where you want to go.
