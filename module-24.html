The Claude Family: Anthropic's Garage

Anthropic hands you four model names in the picker, Haiku, Sonnet, Opus, Fable, with zero explanation. Here's exactly what each one is for.

In this module

  • What each Claude model is actually built for
  • Which one to reach for by default
  • When Fable or Opus is genuinely worth the wait

The problem

Four names, zero explanation.

You open Claude, tap the model picker, and see four unfamiliar words staring back. No hint which one you're supposed to use, or why there are four in the first place.

Good news: once you know the vehicle analogy from the last lesson, this list makes itself obvious.

The core analogy

Anthropic's Garage

Anthropic's fleet, smallest to biggest: Haiku is the bike, fast, cheap, built for quick errands. Sonnet is the sedan, your daily driver, comfortable for almost everything. Opus is the moving truck, slower and pricier, but it can haul a genuinely hard problem. Fable is the full-size moving van, Anthropic's biggest, most capable model, for the heaviest, most complex jobs.

Same fleet idea as always, just with Anthropic's actual names on the doors.

Names bump their version numbers every few months (today it's Haiku 4.5, Sonnet 4.6, Opus 4.8, Fable 5). Ignore the numbers, remember which vehicle each one is.

How it actually works

What each one is actually for

Haiku (the bike) answers almost instantly and costs the least. Perfect for quick chat, simple rewrites, summarizing a short email, anything where "good enough, fast" beats "perfect, slow."

Sonnet, the one you'll use most

Sonnet (the sedan) is the best balance of quality, speed, and cost for the vast majority of real work: drafting, analysis, planning, most of what fills an actual workday. If you're not sure which to pick, Sonnet is almost always the right default.

Opus, for genuinely hard problems

Opus (the truck) thinks longer and harder. Reach for it on complex reasoning, messy multi-step analysis, or anything where being right matters more than being fast.

Fable, the flagship

Fable (the moving van) is Anthropic's most capable model, built for the biggest, most complex, most multi-step work, the kind of job where an AI agent has to plan, use tools, and carry a task across many steps on its own. Most everyday work will never need it, but when a job is genuinely that big, it's the one that can actually carry it.

Rate limits reward picking small

On a Claude subscription, your message allowance stretches much further on Haiku or Sonnet than on Opus or Fable. Save the big engines for the jobs that actually need them.

What this means for you

Which one to actually tap

1. Start every day on Sonnet

It's the daily driver for a reason. Default here unless you have a specific reason not to.

2. Drop to Haiku for anything trivial

A quick question, a one-line rewrite, a fast summary, Haiku handles it instantly and saves your Sonnet/Opus quota for later.

3. Bump up to Opus or Fable when it's genuinely hard

A gnarly analysis, a big messy document, a multi-step project you want an AI to actually carry, that's truck-and-van territory. Worth the wait.

Try it this week

Next time you open Claude, check the model picker before you type. Match it to the size of the task, don't just leave it on default out of habit.

One takeaway

Say it to a colleague

Haiku is the bike, Sonnet the daily driver, Opus the truck, Fable the moving van. Default to Sonnet, and reach for the others only when the job actually needs them.
